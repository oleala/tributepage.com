Steven Paul Jobs was an American inventor,designer and entrepreneur who was the co-founder and chairman of Apple Computer. Jobs was born on February 24, 1955, in San Francisco, California. He lived with his adoptive family in California,within the area thatwould later become known as Silicon Valley While Jobs was always an intelligent and innovative thinker, his youth was riddled with frustrations over formal schooling.After high school, Jobs enrolled at Reed College in Oregon. Lacking direction, he dropped out of college after six months and spent the next 18 months dropping in on creative classes at the school. Back when Jobs was enrolled at High School he was introduced to his future partner of Apple Computer, Wozniak.In 1976 Steve and Wozniak started Apple Computer in a garage Jobs and Wozniak are credited with revolutionizing the computer industry with Apple by democratizing the technology and making machines smaller, cheaper, and accessible to everyday consumers.Jobs left the company in 1985,launching Pixar Animation Studios, then returned to Apple more than a decade later. Jobs died in 2011 following a long battle with pancreatic cancer.
